# US H1-B Visa — Embassy Documentation Rules

## Required Documents
1. **DS-160 Confirmation Page** — Must be printed with barcode visible. Missing this blocks scheduling of the interview.
2. **Form I-797 Approval Notice** — Original, not photocopy. This is the primary proof that USCIS approved the petition; without it the consular officer cannot verify petition status in the system.
3. **Valid Passport** — Must have at least 6 months validity beyond intended stay. Passports expiring within 6 months are rejected because the visa sticker requires a valid page margin period.
4. **Photograph** — 2x2 inch, white background, taken within last 6 months. Photos with shadows or non-white backgrounds are the #1 rejection reason at the visa photo counter.
5. **Employment Verification Letter** — From petitioning employer, on letterhead, signed, stating job title, salary, and duties. Missing this means the officer cannot confirm the specialty occupation criteria.
6. **Educational Credentials** — Degree certificates + transcripts, with WES/ECE evaluation if from a non-US institution. Required to prove the "specialty occupation" degree requirement under INA 214(i)(1).
7. **Pay Stubs / Tax Returns (if applicable)** — For extension or transfer cases only, last 2 years.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Petition not yet approved (I-797 shows "Receipt", not "Approval") — applicant applied too early.
- Salary in the offer letter is below the prevailing wage listed in the Labor Condition Application (LCA).
- Employment start date is more than 6 months in the future.

## Interview Notes
- Applicants must bring original documents; photocopies alone are grounds for administrative processing delay (221(g)).
