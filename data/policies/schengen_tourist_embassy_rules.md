# Schengen (Germany) Tourist Visa — Embassy Documentation Rules

## Required Documents
1. **Visa Application Form** — Signed on the last page. Unsigned forms are not accepted at submission.
2. **Passport** — Issued within last 10 years, valid for 3+ months beyond planned departure from Schengen area, with 2 blank pages.
3. **Biometric Photo** — Schengen spec (35x45mm), taken in last 3 months.
4. **Travel Insurance** — Minimum coverage of EUR 30,000, valid across all Schengen states, covering the full trip duration including repatriation. Missing or insufficient coverage is an automatic rejection.
5. **Flight Reservation** — Round-trip, dated, in applicant's name (not necessarily purchased, a hold/reservation is acceptable in most consulates).
6. **Hotel Booking / Invitation Letter** — Proof of accommodation for every night of the stay. If staying with a host, a notarized invitation letter ("Verpflichtungserklärung") is required instead.
7. **Proof of Financial Means** — Bank statements for last 3 months showing sufficient funds (approx EUR 45/day of stay), or a sponsor's statement + sponsorship letter.
8. **Employment Proof** — Leave-approval letter and salary slips (last 3 months), to demonstrate strong ties to home country and intent to return.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Insufficient bank balance or large unexplained deposits right before application (raises suspicion of borrowed funds).
- Travel insurance policy excludes Schengen-wide coverage or has a coverage gap on specific dates.
- No proof of ties to home country (job, property, family) — used to assess "intent to return."
